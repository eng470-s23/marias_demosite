Women Replace Men.  Seattle, Wash. — Encroachment of women in jobs formerly occupied by men is shown in a report just issued by the Seattle chamber of commerce. In 1920, in the State of Washington, there were 581 women barbers, hair­dressers and manicurists; 48 women fishermen and oystermen; seven wom­en dairymen, teamsters and expressmen; five women longshoremen; 24 women laborers in shipyards; 261 build­ing laborers. 233 sawmill and planing mill laborers and 53 lumbermen, raftsmen and woodchoppers.
