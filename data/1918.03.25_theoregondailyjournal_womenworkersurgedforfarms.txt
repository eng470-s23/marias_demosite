Women Workers Urged For Farms Senate Agricultural Committee Hears Experience of Canada  Under War Conditions.  WASHINGTON, March 2S. (U. P.) The enrolling of a vast army of  women workers to help raise the corps of the nation was today advocated by Miss St. John (Wileman of Ottawa, Canada, who appeared before the senate  agricultural committee. She had been active in her own country in enlisting women farm workers.  "The time is ripe for tapping the great reservoir of women labor for the farms as we have in Canada," she declared. "It has been demonstrated that, three woman can accomplish the same amount of work as two men and they are often more efficient and stick to their jobs better.  Statistics both in Canada and England showed that the women have suffered no ill effets from their work. Instead, they are fat, ruddy ana of good physique."
