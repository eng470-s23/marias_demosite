450 Drowned As Chinese Vessel Sinks In Yangtze SHANGHAI, Oct. 16 (AP) Authorities estimated 450 Chinese men and women laborers drowned today when the overloaded 180-ton river vessel Hsin Taiku Maru capsized in the storm-swept Yangtze estuary 12 miles from Woosung. The only foreigners aboard, a Japanese captain, was believed lost with his ship.  Several vessels in the vicinity reported rough water made rescue attempt almost impossible. The passengers on the boat were employed in Japanese-dominated areas along both banks of the Yangtze.
