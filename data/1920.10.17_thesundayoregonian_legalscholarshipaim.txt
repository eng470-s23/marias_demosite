LEGAL SCHOLARSHIP AIM Students at University Urged to Do Independent Work. UNIVERSITY OF OREGON, Eugene. Oct. 16 (Special.)- That the object of the University of Oregon law school is not merely to develop lawyers but to encourage real scholarship as well, is evidenced by the research work now being done. The work is under the supervision of Professors Larremore, Warner and Dalzell. It is Intended primarily for third year students, and consists of certain problems in law. According to Professor Dalzell such topics as "Women's Labor Laws and How They Are Evaded" are among those now being studied. In this special topic the status of Oregon will be considered to a great extent.
